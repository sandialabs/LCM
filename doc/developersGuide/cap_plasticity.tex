\begin{abstract}

  LCM implements two Cap-plasticity constitutive models,
  \texttt{CapExplicit} and \texttt{CapImplicit}, as specializations
  of the three-invariant isotropic/kinematic hardening cap model of
  \citet{Fossum.Fredrich:2000} as presented in Section~3 of
  \citet{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014}. This document restates the
  formulation in that paper's notation, documents the two integration
  algorithms as coded in LCM, describes the logarithmic-strain /
  Kirchhoff-stress kinematic extension used in LCM for finite
  deformation (an LCM-specific extension not covered by the
  reference), and summarizes why the consistent tangent is never
  formed explicitly: all derivatives needed by the global nonlinear
  solve and by sensitivity analysis are produced by Sacado automatic
  differentiation.
  \\
  \\
  Keywords: Cap plasticity, three-invariant yield surface, kinematic
  hardening, logarithmic strain, Kirchhoff stress, automatic
  differentiation, LCM.

\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and Reference}
\label{sec:scope}

LCM provides two Cap-plasticity constitutive models:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{\texttt{CapExplicit}} --- refined explicit integration
      with an initial plastic predictor followed by a drift-correction
      sub-iteration (\citealt{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014}, Algorithms~1--2).
\item \textbf{\texttt{CapImplicit}} --- fully implicit backward-Euler
      integration of the same yield and plastic-potential functions
      via a 13-unknown local Newton residual, with the local Jacobian
      formed by forward-mode automatic differentiation
      (\texttt{Sacado\bcc Fad\bcc DFad}).
\end{itemize}
Both models share \texttt{compute\_f}, \texttt{compute\_g}, their
first derivatives with respect to the invariants, the kinematic
hardening rule $\halpha$, and the cap hardening function
$\varepsilon_v^p(\kappa)$ verbatim; they differ only in the return
mapping and, through a \texttt{Finite Deformation} input flag,
in kinematics. The paper itself is restricted to small strain;
the extension in Section~\ref{sec:finite} is implemented in LCM but
not documented in \citet{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014}.

\section{Kinematics in the Small-Strain Path}
\label{sec:smallstrain}

The small-strain path additively decomposes the strain rate
\citep[Eq.~47]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014},
\begin{equation}
  \dot{~\varepsilon} \;=\; \dot{\estrain} + \dot{\pstrain},
\end{equation}
and uses isotropic linear elasticity
\citep[Eqs.~49--50]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014},
\begin{equation}
  \dot{\stress} \;=\; \Celast : \dot{\estrain}
    \;=\; \Celast : (\dot{~\varepsilon} - \dot{\pstrain}),
  \qquad
  \Celast \;=\; \lambda~I \otimes ~I + 2\mu ~{\mathbb I},
\end{equation}
with $\lambda,\mu$ the Lam\'e constants and $~{\mathbb I}$ the
symmetric fourth-order identity. Given state
$\{\stress_n, \backstress_n, \kappa_n\}$ and strain increment
$\strainincr$ from the surrounding Newton iteration, the trial
elastic stress is
\begin{equation}
  \stress^{\mathrm{tr}} \;=\; \stress_n + \Celast : \strainincr .
  \label{eq:trial_small}
\end{equation}

\section{Invariants, Lode Coefficient, and Yield Surface}
\label{sec:yield}

Kinematic hardening is introduced through the deviatoric back stress
$\backstress$ \citep[Section~3.1]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014} via the
relative stress
\begin{equation}
  \relstress \;:=\; \stress - \backstress.
\end{equation}
Its invariants are
\begin{align}
  I_1        &= \trace \relstress, \\
  J_2^{\xi}  &= \tfrac{1}{2}\, \sdev : \sdev,
                \qquad \sdev \;=\; \relstress - \tfrac{1}{3} I_1 ~I, \\
  J_3^{\xi}  &= \det \sdev .
\end{align}
Because $\backstress$ is deviatoric,
$\trace\relstress = \trace\stress$, so the $\xi$ superscript is used
on $J_2, J_3$ only.

\paragraph{Lode coefficient.}
To capture the difference in strength between triaxial compression
and extension \citep[Eqs.~34--36]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014},
\begin{equation}
  \beta \;=\; -\tfrac{1}{3}\,
      \sin^{-1}\!\left(
        \frac{3\sqrt{3}\,J_3^\xi}{2\,(J_2^\xi)^{3/2}}
      \right),
  \qquad
  \Gamma(\beta) \;=\;
    \tfrac{1}{2}\left[
      1 - \frac{3\sqrt{3}\,J_3^\xi}{2(J_2^\xi)^{3/2}}
    + \frac{1}{\psi}\!\left(
        1 + \frac{3\sqrt{3}\,J_3^\xi}{2(J_2^\xi)^{3/2}}
      \right)
    \right],
  \label{eq:Gamma}
\end{equation}
with $\psi$ the ratio of triaxial-extension to -compression strength.
Both LCM models use the second form of \eqref{eq:Gamma} directly.
They set $\Gamma \equiv 1$ when $\psi = 0$ or $J_2^\xi = 0$ to avoid
the singularity.

\paragraph{Shear failure surface, cap, and hardening.}
The pressure-dependent shear failure surface and the smooth
elliptical hardening cap of
\citet[Eqs.~32, 37--38]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014} are
\begin{align}
  F_f(I_1) &= A - C \exp(D\,I_1) - \theta\,I_1,
             \label{eq:Ff}\\
  F_c(I_1,\kappa) &=
    1 - H(\kappa - I_1)\,\frac{(I_1 - \kappa)^2}{[X(\kappa) - \kappa]^2},
             \label{eq:Fc}\\
  X(\kappa) &= \kappa - R\,F_f(\kappa),
             \label{eq:X}
\end{align}
where $H$ is the Heaviside function, so $F_c \equiv 1$ on the shear
branch $I_1 > \kappa$ and $F_c < 1$ on the compactive cap
$I_1 \le \kappa$. Sign convention follows the reference: tension
positive, compression negative; the cap sits on the compressive side.
The material parameters are $A, C, D, \theta$ for the shear surface,
$R$ for the cap aspect ratio, and $N$ for the offset of the yield
surface from $F_f$.

\paragraph{Yield function and plastic potential.}
\citet{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014} handle non-associativity by using a
separate copy of $F_f, F_c, X$ for the plastic potential, with
parameters $(L, \phi, Q)$ replacing $(D, \theta, R)$
\citep[Eqs.~30--33, 39--40]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014}:
\begin{align}
  f(I_1, J_2^\xi, J_3^\xi, \backstress, \kappa)
    &= \Gamma^2 J_2^\xi - F_c(\kappa)\,(F_f(I_1) - N)^2,
    \label{eq:f}\\
  g(I_1, J_2^\xi, J_3^\xi, \backstress, \kappa)
    &= \Gamma^2 J_2^\xi - F_c^g(\kappa)\,(F_f^g(I_1) - N)^2,
    \label{eq:g}\\
  F_f^g(I_1)  &= A - C \exp(L\,I_1) - \phi\,I_1, \\
  F_c^g(I_1,\kappa) &=
    1 - H(\kappa - I_1)\,\frac{(I_1 - \kappa)^2}{[X^g(\kappa) - \kappa]^2}, \\
  X^g(\kappa) &= \kappa - Q\,F_f^g(\kappa).
\end{align}
Associative flow corresponds to $L = D$, $\phi = \theta$, $Q = R$.

\section{Flow Rule and Hardening Evolution}
\label{sec:flow}

The plastic strain rate follows the gradient of the plastic
potential \citep[Eq.~48]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014},
\begin{equation}
  \dot{\pstrain} \;=\; \dot\gamma\,\frac{\partial g}{\partial \stress},
  \label{eq:flow}
\end{equation}
where $\dot\gamma$ is the consistency parameter. The deviatoric back
stress evolves along the deviator of the flow direction
\citep[Eqs.~41--43]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014},
\begin{equation}
  \dot{\backstress} \;=\; \dot\gamma\,\halpha(\backstress),
  \qquad
  \halpha \;=\; c^{\alpha}\,G^{\alpha}(\backstress)\,
               \dev\!\left(\frac{\partial g}{\partial \stress}\right),
  \qquad
  G^{\alpha}(\backstress) \;=\; 1 - \frac{\sqrt{J_2^{\alpha}}}{N},
  \label{eq:halpha}
\end{equation}
with $J_2^{\alpha} = \tfrac{1}{2}\backstress:\backstress$.
Isotropic cap hardening is driven by volumetric plastic strain
\citep[Eqs.~44--46]{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014},
\begin{equation}
  \dot\kappa \;=\; \dot\gamma\,h^{\kappa},
  \qquad
  h^{\kappa} \;=\;
    \frac{\trace(\partial g/\partial \stress)}
         {\partial \varepsilon_v^p/\partial \kappa},
  \qquad
  \varepsilon_v^p(\kappa)
    = W \bigl[
        \exp\!\bigl(D_1(X - X_0) - D_2(X - X_0)^2\bigr) - 1
      \bigr],
  \label{eq:kappa}
\end{equation}
where $X_0 = X(\kappa_0)$ and $W, D_1, D_2, c^\alpha$ are material
parameters. In LCM the denominator in $h^{\kappa}$ is evaluated as
$\partial \varepsilon_v^p/\partial X \cdot \mathrm{d}X/\mathrm{d}\kappa$
(function \texttt{compute\_dedkappa}), matching the chain rule
implicit in \eqref{eq:kappa}.

\section{Explicit Integration --- \texttt{CapExplicit}}
\label{sec:explicit}

\texttt{CapExplicit} implements Algorithms~1--2 of
\citet{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014} at a single quadrature point with
input state $\{\stress_n, \backstress_n, \kappa_n\}$ and strain
increment $\strainincr$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Elastic trial} via~\eqref{eq:trial_small};
      $\backstress^{\mathrm{tr}} = \backstress_n$,
      $\kappa^{\mathrm{tr}} = \kappa_n$.
\item Evaluate $f^{\mathrm{tr}} =
      f(\stress^{\mathrm{tr}}, \backstress^{\mathrm{tr}},
        \kappa^{\mathrm{tr}})$. If $f^{\mathrm{tr}} \le 0$, accept
      the elastic trial and stop.
\item \emph{Initial plastic predictor} with derivatives evaluated at
      $\stress_n$ (the pre-trial stress),
      \begin{equation}
        \Delta\gamma \;=\;
        \frac{(\partial f/\partial \stress) : \Celast : \strainincr}
             {\chi},
        \qquad
        \chi \;=\; \tfrac{\partial f}{\partial \stress}
                  :\Celast:\tfrac{\partial g}{\partial \stress}
                - \tfrac{\partial f}{\partial \backstress}:\halpha
                - \tfrac{\partial f}{\partial \kappa}\,h^{\kappa}.
        \label{eq:dgamma_pred}
      \end{equation}
      Update
      $\stress \gets \stress^{\mathrm{tr}}
        - \Delta\gamma\,\Celast:\partial g/\partial \stress$,
      $\backstress \gets \backstress^{\mathrm{tr}}
        + \Delta\gamma\,\halpha$,
      $\kappa \gets \kappa^{\mathrm{tr}}
        + \min(0,\,\Delta\gamma\,h^{\kappa})$;
      the $\min$ enforces the monotonicity requirement that the cap
      hardens only toward the origin.
\item \emph{Stress-drift correction} (Algorithm~2). Repeat while
      $|f| > $ tol and iter $<$ max$_{\mathrm{iter}}$:
      \begin{enumerate}
      \item $\delta\gamma \gets f/\chi$ with $\chi$ re-evaluated at
            the current $\{\stress, \backstress, \kappa\}$.
      \item Form the trial update $\{\stress^k, \backstress^k,
            \kappa^k\}$ via the same rules as Step~3.
      \item If the trial increases $|f|$, fall back to a pure
            \emph{normal correction}:
            $\delta\gamma = f/\|\partial f/\partial \stress\|^2$,
            update only $\stress \gets \stress - \delta\gamma\,
            \partial f/\partial \stress$, hold
            $\backstress, \kappa$ fixed.
      \item Commit the update; accumulate $\Delta\gamma$.
      \end{enumerate}
\item Recover the plastic strain increment through elastic compliance,
      \begin{equation}
        \Delta\pstrain \;=\; (\Celast)^{-1} : (\stress^{\mathrm{tr}}
                                              - \stress_{n+1}),
      \end{equation}
      and update the history variables
      $\varepsilon_v^p \mathrel{+}= \trace\Delta\pstrain$ and
      $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{eqps}} \mathrel{+}=
      \sqrt{\tfrac{2}{3}}\,\|\dev\Delta\pstrain\|$.
\end{enumerate}

The derivatives $\partial f/\partial \stress$,
$\partial g/\partial \stress$, $\partial f/\partial \kappa$, and
$\halpha$ needed by the corrector are provided in closed form
in \texttt{CapExplicit\_Def.hpp}
(\texttt{compute\_dfdsigma}, \texttt{compute\_dgdsigma},
\texttt{compute\_dfdkappa}, \texttt{compute\_halpha}). These are the
only hand-coded derivatives in the explicit path.

\section{Implicit Integration --- \texttt{CapImplicit}}
\label{sec:implicit}

\texttt{CapImplicit} replaces the explicit corrector by a fully
implicit backward-Euler step. The unknown vector packs the symmetric
stress (6 components), the deviatoric back stress (5 independent
components; $\alpha_{33} = -\alpha_{11} - \alpha_{22}$), the cap
parameter, and the plastic multiplier:
\begin{equation}
  \unkvec \;=\;
  \bigl(\sigma_{11}, \sigma_{22}, \sigma_{33},
        \sigma_{23}, \sigma_{13}, \sigma_{12},
        \alpha_{11}, \alpha_{22},
        \alpha_{23}, \alpha_{13}, \alpha_{12},
        \kappa, \Delta\gamma\bigr), \qquad \dim \unkvec = 13.
\end{equation}
The backward-Euler residual $\resvec(\unkvec) = ~0$ reads
\begin{align}
  \resvec_\sigma&\!: \ \
    \stress - \stress^{\mathrm{tr}}
    + \Delta\gamma\,\Celast : \frac{\partial g}{\partial \stress}
    \;=\; ~0, \label{eq:R_sigma}\\
  \resvec_\alpha&\!: \ \
    \backstress - \backstress_n
    - \Delta\gamma\,\halpha \;=\; ~0, \label{eq:R_alpha}\\
  R_\kappa&\!: \ \
    \kappa - \kappa_n - \Delta\gamma\,h^{\kappa} \;=\; 0,
    \label{eq:R_kappa}\\
  R_\gamma&\!: \ \
    f(\stress, \backstress, \kappa) \;=\; 0. \label{eq:R_gamma}
\end{align}
Equations \eqref{eq:R_sigma}--\eqref{eq:R_gamma} are solved by local
Newton iteration. A monotonicity guard $\Delta\kappa \le 0$ is applied
at each step (reject the Newton step and hold $\kappa$ fixed if it
would increase).

\paragraph{Local Jacobian via automatic differentiation.}
Inside \texttt{compute\_ResidJacobian} the 13 unknowns are promoted
to \texttt{Sacado\bcc Fad\bcc DFad} dual numbers, and the residual
\eqref{eq:R_sigma}--\eqref{eq:R_gamma} is evaluated in a single pass
with $f$, $g$, and $\partial g/\partial \stress$ computed from the
templated kernels \texttt{compute\_f}, \texttt{compute\_g},
\texttt{compute\_dgdsigma}. The $13 \times 13$ local Jacobian
$\partial \resvec/\partial \unkvec$ is read directly off the Fad
partial derivatives. No closed-form derivatives of the residual are
written in the implicit path.

\section{Finite-Deformation Extension}
\label{sec:finite}

\citet{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014} state the constitutive model in the
small-strain regime. LCM extends both \texttt{CapExplicit} and
\texttt{CapImplicit} to finite deformation by a multiplicative
elastic/plastic split combined with a logarithmic-strain /
Kirchhoff-stress representation,
\begin{equation}
  \Fdef \;=\; ~F^e\,\Fplas,
  \qquad
  \bee \;=\; \Fdef\,\Fplas^{-1}\,(\Fplas^{-1})^{\mathrm{T}}\,\transpose{\Fdef},
\end{equation}
so that the algorithm of Sections~\ref{sec:explicit}
and~\ref{sec:implicit} is run verbatim on the Kirchhoff stress
$\kirch$. At each quadrature point with incoming $\Fdef$ and stored
$\Fplas_n$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Trial elastic left Cauchy--Green tensor} from the stored
      plastic configuration,
      \begin{equation}
        \bee^{\mathrm{tr}} \;=\;
        \Fdef\,(\Fplas)_n^{-1}\,((\Fplas)_n^{-1})^{\mathrm{T}}\,\transpose{\Fdef}.
      \end{equation}
\item \emph{Trial logarithmic elastic strain},
      \begin{equation}
        \estrain^{\mathrm{tr}} \;=\;
        \tfrac{1}{2}\,\log_{\mathrm{sym}}\bee^{\mathrm{tr}},
        \label{eq:logstrain}
      \end{equation}
      with $\log_{\mathrm{sym}}$ the symmetric principal logarithm.
\item \emph{Trial Kirchhoff stress},
      \begin{equation}
        \kirch^{\mathrm{tr}} \;=\;
        \lambda\,\trace(\estrain^{\mathrm{tr}})\,~I
        + 2\mu\,\estrain^{\mathrm{tr}}.
      \end{equation}
\item \emph{Run the small-strain return mapping of
      Section~\ref{sec:explicit} or~\ref{sec:implicit} on} $\kirch$.
      Because the invariants $I_1, J_2^\xi, J_3^\xi$, the Lode
      coefficient $\Gamma$, the surfaces $F_f, F_c, X$, the flow
      direction $\partial g/\partial \stress$, and the hardening
      functions $\halpha, h^{\kappa}$ are all isotropic functions of
      the stress tensor, the return-mapping-in-principal-log-strain
      result of \citet{Simo:1992} applies: the integrator of
      Section~\ref{sec:explicit} (or the implicit residual
      \eqref{eq:R_sigma}--\eqref{eq:R_gamma}) is run with
      $\kirch, \estrain$ in the slots that previously held
      $\stress, ~\varepsilon$, without any change to the return map
      or hardening code.
\item \emph{Plastic deformation-gradient update} by the exponential
      map of the converged flow direction,
      \begin{equation}
        \Fplas_{n+1} \;=\;
        \exp\!\left(
          \Delta\gamma\,\frac{\partial g}{\partial \kirch}\bigg|_{n+1}
        \right) \Fplas_n .
        \label{eq:Fp_update}
      \end{equation}
\item \emph{Cauchy stress} recovered for assembly,
      \begin{equation}
        \stress_{n+1} \;=\; \kirch_{n+1} / J,
        \qquad J \;=\; \det\Fdef.
      \end{equation}
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The small-strain and finite-deformation branches share
\texttt{compute\_f}, \texttt{compute\_g}, \texttt{compute\_dfdsigma},
\texttt{compute\_dgdsigma}, \texttt{compute\_halpha},
\texttt{compute\_dedkappa}, and \texttt{compute\_dfdkappa} without
modification; only the kinematic pre/post-processing
(Steps~1--3, 5--6 above) and the stored state variables
(\texttt{Strain}/\texttt{Strain\_old} vs.\ \texttt{F}/\texttt{Fp}
plus \texttt{Fp\_old}) differ.

\section{Tangents Are Not Needed: Automatic Differentiation}
\label{sec:ad}

\citet{Sun.Chen.Ostien:2014} present the constitutive equations but
do not document the consistent algorithmic tangent
$\partial \stress_{n+1}/\partial ~\varepsilon_{n+1}$, nor the
finite-deformation counterpart $\partial \kirch/\partial \Fdef$.
\textbf{LCM does not need either tangent}, for three related reasons.

First, LCM's evaluators are templated on an evaluation trait. For
the \texttt{PHAL\bcc AlbanyTraits\bcc Jacobian} trait, all fields --- the
deformation gradient $\Fdef$, the strain tensor, the stored state
inputs, and every intermediate quantity in the return mapping ---
carry a Sacado forward-mode AD type whose active directions are the
element-level global degrees of freedom. When the Cap kernels execute
under this trait, the entire return-mapping code path (elastic
trial, yield check, corrector iterations, $\Fplas$ update, Cauchy
conversion) is run in AD arithmetic. The derivative of the output
stress with respect to every element DOF is produced as a by-product
of the primal evaluation. This is the global analogue of an
algorithmic consistent tangent, generated without any tangent-
specific code path.

Second, inside \texttt{CapImplicit} the local 13-equation
backward-Euler solve \eqref{eq:R_sigma}--\eqref{eq:R_gamma} requires
the local Jacobian $\partial \resvec/\partial \unkvec$. LCM runs
the residual assembly in a nested inner AD (\texttt{DFadType}) of
dimension 13 and harvests the local Jacobian directly from the Fad
partials. Global sensitivities are then propagated through the
converged local solution by
\texttt{LocalNonlinearSolver\bcc computeFadInfo}, which applies the
implicit-function theorem automatically so that the outer (global)
Fad seeds are recovered correctly.

Third, sensitivities with respect to material parameters (for
calibration or inverse problems) are generated by the same
mechanism: material parameters registered through Albany's
distributed parameter library are promoted to AD active directions,
and their derivatives flow through the Cap kernels verbatim.

The only derivatives that are hand-coded in either Cap model are the
physical ones: the flow direction $\partial g/\partial \stress$, the
hardening slopes $\halpha, h^{\kappa},
\partial \varepsilon_v^p/\partial \kappa$, and (in the explicit
path only) $\partial f/\partial \stress$ and
$\partial f/\partial \kappa$. These are \emph{constitutive
derivatives} --- part of the definition of the model --- not
\emph{algorithmic} derivatives of the integrator. All algorithmic
tangents (local, global, consistent, parameter-sensitivity) are
produced by AD.
